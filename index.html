  • Children Services & Protective Services

    Emergency: To report child abuse or neglect, or abuse of an adult age 60+, call 567-242-2700. A caseworker remains on call after office hours. Emergency situations can also be reported to law enforcement.

    Child Protective Services

    Mission: To improve the safety, permanency, and well-being of children and families involved in the Child Welfare system through collaboration with families and their community.

    How to Report: Call Auglaize County Children Services at 567-242-2700 and state you wish to make a child abuse and neglect report.

    Confidentiality: The law protects the confidentiality of a reporter. A social worker cannot reveal, confirm or deny who a reporter may be. There are no consequences for reports made in good faith, even if unfounded.

    Adult Protective Services

    Adult Protective Services staff investigate reports of abuse, neglect (including self-neglect) or financial exploitation of adults age 60 and over who are unable to protect themselves due to a serious physical or mental limitation.

    Community Partners:

    • Auglaize County Council on Aging: 419-394-8252
    • Area Agency on Aging 3: 419-222-7723

    Foster Care & Adoption

    Become a Foster Parent: Single or married, homeowners or renters, age 21+. Must be licensed with ongoing training and criminal check required.

    Adoption Requirements: Single or married, homeowners or renters, age 21+. If married, must have been married for at least one year.

    Safe Haven for Newborns

    Birth parents may leave an infant (up to 30 days old) with a medical worker at a hospital, emergency medical worker, or law enforcement officer without prosecution if the infant has not been abused.
